Mission Statement

Institutional Mission

E5 Enclave is a Think Tank convening Coalitions of the Willing to build the institutions, evidence systems, collaborative platforms, and community projects needed to advance the social, economic, educational, environmental, civic, and emancipated future of the lineage.

Through research, public education, technology-enabled coordination, coalition fundraising, and project incubation, E5 supports organizations and leaders working to repair inherited harm and build durable Black-led institutions.

Public Mission

The Public Call

E5 builds Coalitions of the Willing — organizations, builders, educators, farmers, technologists, advocates, and community leaders working together to strengthen the future of the lineage.

From Liberty City to the nation, E5 exists to build the evidence, institutions, farms, schools, businesses, media, and policy tools our communities need to repair, rise, and govern their future.

Doctrinal Cadence

Emancipated

The issue is not whether emancipation occurred. The issue is whether the obligations created by emancipation were ever fulfilled.

Emancipation created obligations.

Those obligations were broken.

Broken obligations can be documented.

Documented obligations can become policy.

Policy can become remedy.

Emancipated

Restitution 246

E5’s wage-loss and opportunity-loss framework for the 246 years of stolen labor endured by the lineage under American chattel slavery.

Restitution 246

A disciplined lane.

$120T

A federal restitution mechanism structured around a $120 trillion bond, held in perpetuity, with interest payments directed to the lineage as partial restitution for lost wages and inherited loss of opportunity.

It is not full reparations. It is not compensation for pain and suffering. It is not the total settlement of Black harm in America. It is a wage-loss and opportunity-loss framework.

The Five Questions

Is it a crime against humanity for one person to own another person?

Should a government make laws that allow one citizen to own another?

If the government makes laws that allow one citizen to own another citizen, is that government responsible for the damage to the enslaved citizens?

What’s the number?

Who is eligible?

This is civic education and policy analysis, not partisan campaign activity. The Lineage Foundation — Think Tank serves as the institutional container for this research, policy development, public education, and long-term reparative framework.
